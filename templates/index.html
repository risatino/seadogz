OVERVIEW: An Interactive Comparison Data Report

Is Urbanization On The Decline?

Utilizing LeafletJS and HighChartJS charting libraries, our team aimed to investigate whether or not Urban areas are growing at a faster pace than their Suburban counterparts. In addition to this, we wanted to look deeper into a potential cause of these trends and used employment data from the Bureau of Labor Statistics to inform our analysis further.

Based on our analysis, we discovered the following:

  • In most cases, suburban population and employment growth outperformed urban population growth. The latter, being a potential driver of Suburban sprawl.
  • In addition to this, high growth areas were consolidated primary among the coast and sunbelt. Perhaps an indication that weather and immigration play a role.

Though it requires deeper research, it does appear that Suburban areas are growing at a faster pace than their Urban counterparts and that a potential driver of this is employment growth.
